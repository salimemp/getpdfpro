## Document 1: Privacy Policy

### Must cover
- [ ] **WHO** — Legal entity name, address, country of registration, contact email (privacy@getpdfpro.com), DPO contact
- [ ] **WHAT data we collect** — broken into categories:
  - Account data (email, name, auth provider, profile picture)
  - Usage data (which tools used, file sizes, job types, timestamps)
  - Files (uploaded PDFs, processed outputs) — **explicitly state retention: 24h max, then auto-deleted**
  - AI prompts and responses (cached 24h, then deleted)
  - Billing data (via Stripe/Razorpay — never see card numbers)
  - Device/technical data (IP, browser, OS, referrer)
  - Cookies (analytics only — Plausible, no tracking cookies)
- [ ] **WHY** — legal basis for each category (consent, contract, legitimate interest)
- [ ] **HOW** — encrypted in transit (TLS), encrypted at rest (AES-256), R2 + Supabase
- [ ] **WHO WE SHARE WITH** — subprocessor list (must match your actual stack):
  - Cloudflare (R2 storage, CDN, DNS)
  - Supabase (auth, database)
  - Railway (API hosting)
  - Vercel (web hosting)
  - Upstash (Redis cache)
  - Google (Gemini AI — for AI features only, opt-in)
  - Resend (transactional email)
  - Stripe (payments, global)
  - Razorpay (payments, India)
  - Sentry (error monitoring)
  - Plausible (privacy-friendly analytics)
  - Hostinger (WordPress hosting)
- [ ] **HOW LONG** — retention periods for each data category
- [ ] **YOUR RIGHTS** — access, rectification, erasure, portability, restrict processing, object, withdraw consent, lodge complaint with supervisory authority
- [ ] **CCPA-specific** — Right to Know, Right to Delete, Right to Opt-Out of Sale, Right to Non-Discrimination, "Do Not Sell My Personal Information" link
- [ ] **GDPR-specific** — lawful basis for each processing, DPO contact, EU representative (if not EU-established), Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) for any non-EEA processors, transfer mechanisms
- [ ] **International transfers** — explain how data crosses borders (it will), SCCs used, supplementary measures
- [ ] **Children** — explicitly 16+ (or higher per jurisdiction), COPPA compliance, do not knowingly collect
- [ ] **AI-specific disclosure** — when you use AI features, your data is sent to Google Gemini under their API terms; opt-in only, "AI off" default for free tier
- [ ] **E2E encryption mode (zero-knowledge)** — what it is, what it means, what we CAN and CANNOT do with your data in that mode
- [ ] **Changes to policy** — how users are notified
- [ ] **Contact** — privacy@getpdfpro.com, response within 30 days

### Critical caveats to include
- We are **not** a HIPAA Business Associate unless you sign a separate BAA
- We are **not** SOC 2 certified (yet) but designed for SOC 2 readiness
- Free tier has ads? (No — we're ad-free. State this.)
- We do not sell user data (state this clearly — required by CCPA)
- AI features are opt-in only

## Document 2: Terms of Use

### Must cover
- [ ] **Acceptance** — by using the service, you agree
- [ ] **Eligibility** — 16+ (or jurisdiction's digital consent age)
- [ ] **Service description** — what we provide, 25 PDF tools, AI features
- [ ] **Account responsibilities** — accurate info, password security, account activity
- [ ] **Acceptable use** — separate document OR inline
- [ ] **Prohibited use** — illegal content, malware, harassment, copyright infringement, attempting to break the service
- [ ] **Intellectual property** — your content stays yours, our IP (code, branding) stays ours, license to use uploaded content only to provide the service
- [ ] **Subscription terms** — auto-renew, cancel anytime, refunds policy
- [ ] **Free vs Pro vs Team** — what each tier includes, what changes
- [ ] **AI features** — "AI may produce inaccurate outputs, use at your own risk for non-critical tasks"
- [ ] **Service availability** — "we aim for 99.9% uptime but do not guarantee"
- [ ] **Limitation of liability** — capped at amount paid in last 12 months, no consequential damages
- [ ] **Indemnification** — you indemnify us for misuse
- [ ] **Dispute resolution** — jurisdiction, arbitration clause (US: AAA), class action waiver
- [ ] **Governing law** — pick a state/country. For a US-based SaaS serving global users, Delaware is common. India: pick Maharashtra or Karnataka.
- [ ] **Modifications** — how we update terms
- [ ] **Termination** — we can suspend for ToS violations, you can close account anytime
- [ ] **Contact** — legal@getpdfpro.com

### Subscription-specific clauses
- **Auto-renewal disclosure** — required in many US states (CA: ROSCA, others). Plain English: "Your subscription will auto-renew unless you cancel at least 24 hours before the end of the current period."
- **Cooling-off period** — EU users have 14 days to cancel for refund
- **Refund policy** — pro-rated for annual, no refund for monthly, full refund within 7 days for first-time annual subscribers (industry norm)
- **Price changes** — 30 days notice

### Liability cap
"IN NO EVENT SHALL GETPDFPRO'S TOTAL LIABILITY EXCEED THE GREATER OF (A) THE AMOUNT YOU PAID US IN THE 12 MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM, OR (B) USD $100."

## Document 5: Data Processing Addendum (DPA)

> Required for GDPR compliance if you serve EU customers.

### Must cover
- [ ] **Parties** — you (Data Processor) and customer (Data Controller)
- [ ] **Scope of processing** — list categories of data, purposes
- [ ] **Controller obligations** — customer is the controller
- [ ] **Processor obligations** — you process only on documented instructions
- [ ] **Subprocessor list** — link to current subprocessor list
- [ ] **Subprocessor changes** — 30 days notice, right to object
- [ ] **Security measures** — list your technical & organizational measures (TLS, AES-256, RBAC, audit logs, etc.)
- [ ] **Data subject rights** — assist controller in responding to DSARs
- [ ] **Breach notification** — within 72 hours of awareness
- [ ] **International transfers** — SCCs, supplementary measures
- [ ] **Audit rights** — once per year, with reasonable notice
- [ ] **Return/deletion at end** — controller can request data return or deletion
- [ ] **Liability cap** — same as ToS

## Document 6: HIPAA-specific (if you want to serve healthcare)

### Reality check
- True HIPAA compliance for a SaaS requires:
  - SOC 2 Type II audit ($30K–$100K+)
  - Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with **all** subprocessors (Cloudflare, Supabase, Railway, Vercel, Upstash, Google, Stripe, Razorpay, Resend, Sentry, Hostinger — every single one)
  - Most of these vendors **do not offer BAA at free tier**. You'd need their enterprise plans.
  - Annual security risk assessments
  - Designated Security Officer
  - Workforce HIPAA training
- **This is a $50K–$200K+ investment minimum**
- Most general PDF tools (iLovePDF, Smallpdf, Adobe online) **do not** claim HIPAA compliance for the same reason
- Adobe Acrobat Pro is HIPAA-compliant only via their enterprise on-prem plan

### Recommendation
- **Don't claim HIPAA compliance for MVP / Phase 1–3**
- **Architect for HIPAA-grade security** (you already are — encryption, audit logs, RBAC, encrypted-at-rest)
- **Explicitly state in Privacy Policy + ToS:**
  - "GetPDFPro is not a HIPAA Business Associate. Do not upload Protected Health Information (PHI) to our service unless you have signed a separate BAA with us."
- **Phase 4 (year 2):** pursue HIPAA + SOC 2 Type II as paid enterprise tier feature

### If you do want HIPAA later
- Hire a HIPAA consultant (~$5K–$15K for the gap analysis)
- Implement all 54 Safeguards in 45 CFR § 164.306–318
- Get BAAs from all subprocessors (some may not offer — this can be a deal-breaker)
- Engage a CPA firm for SOC 2 Type II audit

## Document 7: SOC 2 (deferred)

### Recommendation
- Don't claim SOC 2 compliance for MVP
- Be "SOC 2-ready" in your security posture:
  - Document all security policies
  - Implement access controls (already doing via Supabase RLS)
  - Set up audit logging (already doing via Sentry + structured logs)
  - Define incident response (already in SECURITY.md)
  - Vendor risk management (subprocessor list)
- In Privacy Policy / enterprise sales: "We follow SOC 2 Trust Service Criteria. Certification pending."
- **Year 2+:** Engage a CPA firm (Drata, Vanta, or a Big 4), prepare for 3–6 months, audit for 6–12 months, certify.
